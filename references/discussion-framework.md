# Cross-domain Discussion Framework

## Discussion Phase Design

### Phase 1: Preliminary Comparison Analysis

#### Objectives
- Analysts understand other dimensions' preliminary conclusions
- Find consensus points
- Identify potential contradiction points

#### Discussion Questions
1. **Consensus points**: What conclusions do you have in common with other analysts?
2. **Contradiction points**: What conflicts or different views do you have with other analysts?
3. **Inquiry**: Regarding contradiction points, what questions do you want to ask related analysts?

#### Sample Questions
- Financial analyst found profit decline vs Technology analyst found R&D investment changes - what's the correlation?
- User experience officer's satisfaction conclusions vs Competition analyst's competitive threat analysis - how to reconcile?

---

### Phase 2: Deep Inquiry

#### Objectives
- Deeply inquire about contradiction points identified in Phase 1
- Get more information to support judgments
- Verify preliminary assumptions

#### Sample Discussion Questions

**To Financial Analyst:**
- In the profit decline, how much is due to price war? How much is due to cost increases?
- What are the specific reasons for cash flow changes?

**To Competition Analyst:**
- How to quantify the threat level of new entrants?
- How to assess the sustainability of moats?

**To User Analyst:**
- Can high NPS be converted into price moats?
- What are the specific old-customer referral data?

**To Technology Analyst:**
- What's the technology catch-up strategy?
- How does R&D efficiency compare with competitors?

---

### Phase 3: Core Contradiction Collision

#### Objectives
- In-depth debate on the most critical contradiction points
- Draw final conclusions
- Integrate multiple perspectives

#### Typical Contradiction Types

1. **Efficiency vs Investment**
   - Financial view: High efficiency is good
   - Technology view: Insufficient investment may cause lag

2. **Short-term vs Long-term**
   - Financial view: Focus on short-term profitability
   - Strategic view: Needs long-term investment

3. **Stock vs Incremental**
   - User view: Stock user value is high
   - Competition view: Incremental market is more important

4. **Certainty vs Risk**
   - Financial view: Control risk
   - Innovation view: Need to take risks

---

## Discussion Organization Process

### Step 1: Initiate Discussion

```markdown
## Phase 2: Deep Discussion

Based on preliminary analysis, I have some key questions to explore with everyone:

### Question 1: [Specific Question]

[Background]

Please answer:
1. [Specific Question 1]
2. [Specific Question 2]
3. [Specific Question 3]

Please share your perspectives from your professional angle, and also respond to other analysts' views!
```

### Step 2: Summarize Views

When discussion is sufficient, summarize key conclusions:

```markdown
## Discussion Summary

**Consensus Points:**
- [Consensus 1]
- [Consensus 2]

**Contradiction Points:**
- [Contradiction 1]
- [Contradiction 2]

**Final Conclusions:**
- [Conclusion 1]
- [Conclusion 2]
```

## Common Discussion Topic Templates

### About Profitability
- Is scale effect working?
- What's the reason for gross margin changes?
- What's the impact of price war on profitability?

### About Competitive Advantage
- What's the moat? How deep is it?
- Are competitors catching up?
- Is the technology gap widening or narrowing?

### About User Value
- How are user satisfaction and loyalty?
- Can it be converted into premium pricing capability?
- How is old-customer referral data?

### About Management
- What's the quality of strategic decisions?
- Is execution capability strong?
- Is the incentive mechanism effective?

### About Risks
- What are the main risks?
- Are risks controllable?
- What are the potential opportunities?
